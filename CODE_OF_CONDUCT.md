# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We want this to be a place where someone can admit they do not understand
something and get a straight answer. That is the whole point of the project — it
teaches *why* things exist, which means the questions people are embarrassed to
ask are the valuable ones.

We pledge to make participation a harassment-free experience for everyone,
regardless of experience level, age, body size, visible or invisible disability,
ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, education,
socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or
sexual identity and orientation.

## Expected behaviour

- Critique the code, the content or the argument — never the person.
- Assume the other person is competent and acting in good faith.
- **"This is wrong, and here is why"** is welcome. **"This is obvious"** is not —
  it teaches nothing and tells the reader they should not have asked.
- Accept that a decision may be documented and settled. Reopen it with an ADR, not
  with repetition.

## Unacceptable behaviour

- Harassment, insults, or personal or political attacks.
- Sexualised language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Sustained disruption of discussion, or dismissing someone for not knowing
  something.

## Enforcement

Report unacceptable behaviour to the maintainer via a private channel — not a
public issue. Reports are handled confidentially.

Maintainers may remove, edit or reject comments, commits, code, issues and pull
requests that violate this Code of Conduct, and may temporarily or permanently ban
any contributor for behaviour they consider inappropriate.
